## 1. Strategic read

**Lithuania is wide open.** The LT SERPs for AI topics are barely contested: "dirbtinis intelektas" has 33,100 searches/mo at keyword difficulty 2. Most LT keywords have no computed difficulty at all because the SERPs are that thin. A new domain with your E-E-A-T (Shark Tank LT, Latitude59, real founder) can rank in weeks.

**English has one urgent cluster and two durable ones.** The OpenClaw search wave peaked at ~1M/mo in Feb-Mar 2026 and is halving monthly (135K in June). The winnable sub-terms ("what is openclaw" KD 36, "openclaw setup" KD 23, "openclaw tutorial" KD 14) map exactly to your course. This content must ship within weeks or the wave is gone. The durable EN plays are "personal ai assistant" (14,800/mo, KD 12) and the Claude Code / vibe coding long tail ("claude code vs cursor" 8,100/mo at KD 5).

**What we deliberately skip.** LT ecommerce head terms (KD 53-92, shop-listing SERPs) and EN ecommerce terms (KD 33-38, flat) belong to callsy.ai, not the personal site. "Konferencijų pranešėjas" and "motyvacinis pranešėjas" have zero measurable demand, the speaking offer rides on "renginių vedėjas" instead. "Kaip sukurti startuolį" has zero volume, Lithuanians search the noun "startuolis", not the how-to.

**Language insight that shapes all LT copy:** Lithuanians attach "lietuviškai" and "nemokamai" to English product names ("chatgpt lietuviskai" 12,100/mo). For courses, "mokymai" beats "kursai" (320 vs 90). Insider jargon stays English ("vibe coding" 590/mo, no LT term exists).

## 5. Open decision: which language at the root

The master prompt says `/` = EN, `/lt` = LT. With LT-first strategy there is a case for flipping. Trade-off:

**Keep EN at root (recommended):**
- The long-term prize is EN: OpenClaw + personal-AI-assistant clusters dwarf all LT volume combined, and GEO citation by ChatGPT/Perplexity happens overwhelmingly in English.
- Google ranks `/lt/...` pages in Lithuania just as well (proven on callsy.ai locales).
- International speaking invites, investors, and press land on an English homepage.
- Matches the master prompt; no rework later.

**Flip LT to root:** only worth it if the site's primary job for the next year is LT lead-gen and the EN market is an afterthought. Painful to reverse (redirects, hreflang, indexed URLs).

Recommendation: EN at root, LT content ships first. Sequencing is strategy, structure is plumbing.
